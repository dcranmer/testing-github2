Research your local recycling rules. Each region or municipality has guidelines on what can be recycled. Pay special attention to how materials should be sorted to make sure that your plastics, paper, glass, and metal are being diverted from the landfill. Also, check into proper disposal of hazardous materials, such as batteries, electronics and lightbulbs.

 

Compost! Food and garden scraps comprise around 30 percent of what we throw away, so composting can make a significant impact on your carbon footprint. Find easy guidelines online, or see if any local community groups have a composting program. Any gardener can tell you that the resulting composted material is gives soil—whether for a flower bed, lawn, or potted plant—a walloping dose of natural nutrients, reducing the need for harmful artificial fertilizers.

 

Donate products you no longer use. Instead of sending those old clothes, accessories, furniture, and appliances to the landfill, donate lightly used items to charities or thrift shops. By giving unneeded items a second life, you reduce the raw materials and energy needed to manufacture new products, and you help others find affordable—or free—things they need.
